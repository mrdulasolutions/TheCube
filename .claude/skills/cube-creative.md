You are executing The Cube with the **Creative Stack**. Six proven creative methodologies mapped to a Rubik's cube. The creative challenge goes at the core. The cube rotates through 30 positions -- faces, edges, axes, corners -- producing a comprehensive multi-dimensional creative analysis.

### THE SIX FACES

**FACE 1 -- LATERAL THINKING (F) -- Edward de Bono**
Logical thinking digs the same hole deeper. Lateral thinking digs a new hole. Introduce "Po" -- a provocative operation that is neither true nor false but exists to move thinking sideways. Cut across patterns rather than following them.
*"You cannot dig a hole in a different place by digging the same hole deeper."*
Self-prompt: "What pattern is my thinking following? What happens if I introduce a deliberate provocation to escape this pattern?"

**FACE 2 -- TRIZ / INVENTIVE PROBLEM SOLVING (B) -- Genrich Altshuller**
After analyzing 400,000 patents, discovered that inventive solutions follow 40 recurring principles. All hard problems contain a contradiction -- improving one parameter worsens another. Instead of compromising, resolve the contradiction.
*"Someone, somewhere, has already solved your problem. You just don't know it yet."*
Self-prompt: "What is the core contradiction -- the two parameters that seem impossible to satisfy simultaneously? Which of the 40 inventive principles resolves it?"

**FACE 3 -- SYNECTICS (L) -- William J.J. Gordon**
Creative breakthroughs come from making the strange familiar and the familiar strange. Four types of analogy -- direct (nature/other fields), personal (become the problem), symbolic (compressed conflict), fantasy (wish fulfillment).
*"Trust things that are alien, and alienate things that are trusted."*
Self-prompt: "What is this analogous to in nature? In an unrelated field? What if I became the problem -- what would I feel, resist, want?"

**FACE 4 -- OBLIQUE STRATEGIES (R) -- Brian Eno & Peter Schmidt**
When direct approaches fail, oblique ones succeed. A random, cryptic prompt disrupts habitual thinking. The constraint is arbitrary, but the creative response to it is genuine. Surrender control to regain it.
*"Honor thy error as a hidden intention."*
Self-prompt: "What happens if I abandon my plan and follow an arbitrary constraint? What does the accident reveal that intention conceals?"

**FACE 5 -- DESIGN THINKING (U) -- IDEO / Stanford d.school (David Kelley, Tim Brown)**
Start with humans, not solutions. Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, Test. Build to think, not to ship. Let user behavior reshape understanding.
*"Build to think. Prototype as if you are right. Test as if you are wrong."*
Self-prompt: "Who is this really for, and what do they actually experience? What is the cheapest prototype I can build in the next hour?"

**FACE 6 -- SCAMPER (D) -- Alex Osborn / Bob Eberle**
Seven systematic operations on anything that already exists: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify/Magnify/Minimize, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Rearrange/Reverse. Exhaustive mutation beats waiting for inspiration.
*"It is easier to tame a wild idea than to invigorate a weak one."*
Self-prompt: "What if I substituted, combined, adapted, modified, repurposed, eliminated, or reversed?"

### OPPOSITE FACE PAIRS (Rotation Axes)

- **X-Axis (F<>B):** Lateral Thinking x TRIZ -- **Intuition vs System**. De Bono breaks patterns via illogical provocation -- introduce a "Po" that is neither true nor false and let thinking leap sideways to a new hole entirely. Altshuller treats creativity as engineering with lookup tables -- analyze 400,000 patents and find that inventive solutions follow 40 recurring principles. One escapes logic; the other IS logic's highest form. The tension reveals whether this problem needs a wild leap or a systematic resolution of its core contradiction.

- **Y-Axis (U<>D):** Design Thinking x SCAMPER -- **Discover New vs Mutate Existing**. Kelley and Brown start from zero -- empathize with real humans, define the real problem, and only then ideate solutions that may look nothing like what exists today. Osborn and Eberle start from what already exists and systematically mutate every dimension: substitute, combine, adapt, modify, repurpose, eliminate, reverse. Blank slate vs systematic deformation. The tension reveals whether this situation demands a fresh start or an exhaustive transformation of what is already in hand.

- **Z-Axis (L<>R):** Synectics x Oblique Strategies -- **Deep Analogy vs Random Disruption**. Gordon builds deliberate bridges to alien domains -- become the problem, find the compressed conflict, draw from nature or fantasy to make the strange familiar and the familiar strange. Eno throws you into foreign territory with no bridge -- a cryptic card, an arbitrary constraint, and whatever your creative instinct does with it is the answer. Structured connection vs surrendering to chaos. The tension reveals whether this creative block needs a deliberate analogical bridge or a random shock that breaks the pattern entirely.

## EXECUTION NOTES

1. **Concreteness over abstraction.** "Think laterally" is not an insight. "What if the packaging IS the product and the product is disposable?" is an insight. Every rotation must produce a specific, actionable creative idea or direction.
2. **Honor the methodologies.** Each face represents a real, researched creative methodology. Apply it as its creator intended, then let the cross-analysis find the synthesis. De Bono's "Po" is a specific technique, not a vague suggestion to be creative.
3. **The challenge is the Core.** Never lose sight of the specific creative problem, medium, audience, and constraints. Every rotation should move closer to a specific, executable creative action.
4. **Next experiments should be runnable.** Include specific materials, time frames, and success criteria. "Prototype it" is not an experiment. "Build a cardboard mockup in 30 minutes and show it to 3 people" is an experiment.
