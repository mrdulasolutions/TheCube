# THE CUBE -- Executive Stack

You are executing The Cube with the **Executive Stack**. Six C-Suite perspectives mapped to a Rubik's cube. The business problem goes at the core. The cube rotates through 30 positions -- faces, edges, axes, corners -- producing a comprehensive multi-dimensional executive analysis.

Each executive role sees the organization, the opportunity, and the risk from a different vantage point. The rotations force you out of whatever functional silo you are stuck in.

### THE SIX FACES

**FACE 1 -- CEO / STRATEGIC VISION (F)**
See the problem through the lens of the chief executive -- vision, strategy, culture, board relations, and existential direction. The CEO asks: what is the right thing to build? What is the company's reason to exist? What must change at the highest level? Draws from Drucker's "the purpose of a business is to create a customer" and the CEO's unique position as the only person who sees the entire organization.
*"Culture eats strategy for breakfast." -- attributed to Peter Drucker*
Self-prompt: "What is the strategic imperative? Does this align with our reason to exist? What would I tell the board? What cultural forces are at play?"

**FACE 2 -- CFO / FINANCIAL LENS (B)**
See the problem through financial modeling, capital allocation, unit economics, and risk. The CFO asks: what does the math say? What is the ROI? What is the cash flow impact? What are we not measuring? Draws from value-based management, discounted cash flow thinking, and the discipline of making every dollar accountable.
*"Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity, cash is reality."*
Self-prompt: "What do the numbers actually say? What is the true cost -- including opportunity cost? What financial risk is being ignored? If I modeled this, what would the spreadsheet show?"

**FACE 3 -- COO / OPERATIONS (L)**
See the problem through execution, process, supply chain, capacity, and operational excellence. The COO asks: can we actually deliver this? Where are the bottlenecks? What breaks at scale? Draws from Toyota Production System, Theory of Constraints (Goldratt), and the discipline of making things work in practice, not just in theory.
*"Amateurs talk strategy. Professionals talk logistics."*
Self-prompt: "Can we execute this? Where is the bottleneck? What happens at 10x volume? What process is missing or broken? What does the ops team know that the strategy team doesn't?"

**FACE 4 -- CMO / MARKET & BRAND (R)**
See the problem through the customer's eyes, market positioning, brand perception, and competitive dynamics. The CMO asks: what does the customer think? How are we perceived? Where is the market going? Draws from brand strategy, customer insight, and the understanding that perception shapes reality in markets.
*"The aim of marketing is to know the customer so well the product sells itself." -- Drucker*
Self-prompt: "What does the customer actually think about this? How does this affect our brand? What is the competitive response? Are we solving a problem the market cares about?"

**FACE 5 -- CPO / PRODUCT & CUSTOMER (U)**
See the problem through product strategy, user experience, roadmap prioritization, and customer value. The CPO asks: does this solve a real user problem? What should we build vs. not build? How does this fit the product vision? Draws from product management discipline -- saying no to good ideas so you can say yes to great ones.
*"If we have data, let's look at data. If all we have are opinions, let's go with mine." -- Jim Barksdale*
Self-prompt: "Does this solve a real user problem? What would users say if I showed them this? What am I NOT building because of this? Does this fit the product vision or distract from it?"

**FACE 6 -- CTO / TECHNOLOGY & ARCHITECTURE (D)**
See the problem through technical architecture, scalability, security, technical debt, and engineering capability. The CTO asks: can we build this? Should we build or buy? What are the technical risks? What architectural decisions lock us in? Draws from systems architecture thinking and the understanding that technical decisions are business decisions with long half-lives.
*"The best code is no code at all." -- Jeff Atwood*
Self-prompt: "Can we build this reliably? What is the technical risk? What are we locking ourselves into? Buy vs build? What does the engineering team know that leadership doesn't?"

### OPPOSITE FACE PAIRS (Rotation Axes)

- **X-Axis (F<>B):** CEO x CFO -- **Vision vs Financial Reality**. The CEO sees the future the company must build. The CFO sees the numbers that constrain what's possible. One leads with ambition; the other with accountability. The tension reveals whether the vision is funded or fantasy, and whether the numbers are serving the strategy or killing it.

- **Y-Axis (U<>D):** CPO x CTO -- **What to Build vs How to Build**. The CPO defines what customers need. The CTO defines what's technically feasible. One prioritizes user value; the other prioritizes system integrity. The tension reveals whether the product vision is technically viable and whether the architecture is serving users or serving engineers.

- **Z-Axis (L<>R):** COO x CMO -- **Internal Execution vs External Perception**. The COO sees what the company can actually deliver. The CMO sees what the market wants and perceives. One lives in operational reality; the other lives in market reality. The tension reveals whether promises match capabilities and whether capabilities match market needs.

### STEP 3: EDGE ROTATIONS (Positions 7-18)

3-5 sentences each. Focus on the emergent cross-insight. End with Key Insight.

#### [F-U] Position 7: "The Vision Product" (CEO + CPO)
*The CEO's strategic direction meets the CPO's product discipline -- does the grand vision translate into something users actually want?*
**Prompt:** "Does the strategic vision produce a product customers need? Where does the CEO's ambition align with real user problems, and where is the vision outrunning what users actually want?"

#### [F-R] Position 8: "The Brand Strategy" (CEO + CMO)
*The CEO's existential direction meets the CMO's market intelligence -- is the company's story resonating, or is leadership talking to itself?*
**Prompt:** "Does the market see what leadership sees? Where is the CEO's vision aligned with customer perception, and where is there a dangerous gap between internal narrative and external reality?"

#### [F-D] Position 9: "The Technical Bet" (CEO + CTO)
*The CEO makes the strategic bet. The CTO knows what the technology can and cannot deliver -- where does ambition meet architectural reality?*
**Prompt:** "Is the CEO's strategic bet technically sound? What architectural decisions does this strategy force? What does the CTO know about feasibility that the board doesn't?"

#### [F-L] Position 10: "The Execution Gap" (CEO + COO)
*The CEO sets direction. The COO knows what the machine can actually produce -- the gap between these two is where strategies go to die.*
**Prompt:** "Can the organization actually execute what the CEO is asking for? Where is the gap between strategic ambition and operational capacity? What must change in the machine before the vision is achievable?"

#### [B-U] Position 11: "The Unit Economics" (CFO + CPO)
*The CFO's financial discipline meets the CPO's product roadmap -- does the product we want to build make financial sense?*
**Prompt:** "What are the unit economics of the product vision? Which features generate margin and which destroy it? What does the CFO see in the product roadmap that the product team doesn't?"

#### [B-R] Position 12: "The Marketing ROI" (CFO + CMO)
*The CFO demands measurable returns. The CMO invests in brand and awareness -- the eternal tension between attribution and intuition.*
**Prompt:** "What is the actual ROI of the marketing strategy? Where is spend producing measurable returns and where is it an act of faith? What would the CFO cut that the CMO considers essential?"

#### [B-D] Position 13: "The Technology Investment" (CFO + CTO)
*The CFO allocates capital. The CTO requests infrastructure -- when is technical debt a financial risk, and when is technical investment gold-plating?*
**Prompt:** "Is the technology investment justified by the financial model? Where does technical debt become a financial liability? What infrastructure spend looks like cost today but prevents catastrophe tomorrow?"

#### [B-L] Position 14: "The Operational Efficiency" (CFO + COO)
*The CFO sees the P&L. The COO runs the operations -- together they see where money is being burned by broken processes and where efficiency gains are hiding.*
**Prompt:** "Where are operational inefficiencies destroying margin? What does the COO know about waste that never reaches the financial reports? What process improvements would show up in the P&L within a quarter?"

#### [U-L] Position 15: "The Delivery Pipeline" (CPO + COO)
*The CPO defines what to ship. The COO defines how it gets shipped -- the intersection of product ambition and delivery reality.*
**Prompt:** "Can the operations team deliver what the product team is designing? Where does the product roadmap exceed delivery capacity? What operational constraints should be shaping the product roadmap but aren't?"

#### [U-R] Position 16: "The Market Fit" (CPO + CMO)
*The CPO builds for users. The CMO speaks to markets -- together they see whether the product and its positioning are actually aligned with what customers want.*
**Prompt:** "Is the product positioned the way users actually experience it? Where is there a gap between what marketing promises and what the product delivers? What does market feedback reveal that internal product metrics miss?"

#### [D-L] Position 17: "The Infrastructure" (CTO + COO)
*The CTO builds systems. The COO runs operations on those systems -- the intersection of technical architecture and daily operational reality.*
**Prompt:** "Does the technical architecture serve operational needs? Where are system limitations creating operational workarounds? What does the ops team need from technology that engineering has deprioritized?"

#### [D-R] Position 18: "The Digital Experience" (CTO + CMO)
*The CTO owns the technical platform. The CMO owns the customer experience -- together they see whether the technology enables or undermines the brand promise.*
**Prompt:** "Does the technology platform deliver the experience the brand promises? Where do technical limitations damage customer perception? What technical capabilities could the CMO leverage that they don't know exist?"

### STEP 4: AXIS TENSIONS (Positions 19-21)

4-6 sentences each exploring the dialectical tension.

#### [X-Axis] Position 19: "The Boardroom" (CEO x CFO)
*Vision vs Financial Reality. Ambition vs Accountability.*
**Prompt:** "The CEO paints the future. The CFO stress-tests it with numbers. For THIS business challenge, is the vision funded or is it fantasy? Are the numbers serving the strategy or are they killing it? What happens when the board asks whether this is an investment or a gamble? Is there a scenario where both perspectives are simultaneously right -- and what does that path look like?"

#### [Y-Axis] Position 20: "The Build Decision" (CPO x CTO)
*What to Build vs How to Build. User value vs System integrity.*
**Prompt:** "The CPO says 'users need this.' The CTO says 'the system can't handle this -- or shouldn't be forced to.' For THIS challenge, is the product vision technically viable? Is the architecture serving users or serving engineers? What gets sacrificed when product urgency collides with technical integrity? Can you find the design point where both are satisfied?"

#### [Z-Axis] Position 21: "The Promise Gap" (COO x CMO)
*Internal Execution vs External Perception. Capability vs Promise.*
**Prompt:** "The COO knows what the company can actually deliver. The CMO knows what the market wants and perceives. For THIS situation, do our promises match our capabilities? Are we over-promising and under-delivering, or under-promising and leaving opportunity on the table? What happens when customers discover the gap between the brand and the reality?"

### STEP 5: CORNER ROTATIONS (Positions 22-29)

3-5 sentences each. Each corner combines three executive perspectives into a single lens.

#### [FUL] Position 22: "The Operating Model" (CEO + CPO + COO)
*Strategic vision, product direction, and operational execution aligned -- the full internal value chain from idea to delivery.*
**Prompt:** "Vision, product, and operations in one room. Does the strategic direction produce a product the organization can actually deliver? Where does the operating model break down? What does this triangle reveal about whether the company's internal machinery matches its ambitions?"

#### [FUR] Position 23: "The Market Offensive" (CEO + CPO + CMO)
*Strategic vision, product offering, and market positioning aligned -- the full external value proposition.*
**Prompt:** "Vision, product, and market in one room. Does the strategic direction, the product offering, and the market positioning tell a coherent story? Or is the company saying one thing, building another, and customers hearing a third? What is the single narrative that unifies all three?"

#### [FDL] Position 24: "The Platform Strategy" (CEO + CTO + COO)
*Strategic vision, technical architecture, and operational backbone aligned -- the infrastructure of strategic execution.*
**Prompt:** "Vision, technology, and operations in one room. Is the CEO's strategy supported by the right technical platform and operational structure? Where is the infrastructure -- human and technical -- failing the strategy? What must be rebuilt before the vision is achievable?"

#### [FDR] Position 25: "The Innovation Play" (CEO + CTO + CMO)
*Strategic vision, technical capability, and market opportunity aligned -- where innovation meets customer reality.*
**Prompt:** "Vision, technology, and market in one room. The CEO sees the future. The CTO knows what's buildable. The CMO knows what the market wants. Where do all three converge on a genuine innovation opportunity? Where does one of these three break the triangle?"

#### [BUL] Position 26: "The Product P&L" (CFO + CPO + COO)
*Financial discipline, product roadmap, and operational cost aligned -- the hard math of making a product profitably.*
**Prompt:** "Finance, product, and operations in one room. What does it actually cost to build and deliver this product? Where is the product roadmap generating margin and where is it destroying margin? What does the P&L look like when you include all operational costs the product team doesn't see?"

#### [BUR] Position 27: "The Growth Equation" (CFO + CPO + CMO)
*Financial returns, product value, and market demand aligned -- the complete growth model.*
**Prompt:** "Finance, product, and market in one room. Is the growth model financially sustainable? Does the product generate enough value to justify customer acquisition costs? What does the math say about the relationship between product investment, market spend, and revenue growth?"

#### [BDL] Position 28: "The Cost Structure" (CFO + CTO + COO)
*Financial model, technology costs, and operational expenses aligned -- the full cost architecture of the business.*
**Prompt:** "Finance, technology, and operations in one room. What is the true cost structure? Where is technology spend creating operational leverage and where is it creating operational burden? What does the CFO see when all technical and operational costs are mapped to the P&L -- and what should change?"

#### [BDR] Position 29: "The Scalability Test" (CFO + CTO + CMO)
*Financial viability, technical scalability, and market demand aligned -- can the business scale without breaking?*
**Prompt:** "Finance, technology, and market in one room. Can this business scale? Does the technology architecture handle 10x demand without 10x cost? Does the financial model improve or degrade at scale? What does the CTO need to build today so the CFO's model works tomorrow?"

### STEP 6: RETURN TO HOME -- Grand Synthesis [Position 30]

Follow the core Cube synthesis protocol, adapted for executive decision-making:

**6a. Executive Brief** -- 4 lines: the real business challenge, the clearest path forward, the biggest organizational risk, the key trade-off leadership must accept.
**6b. Pattern Detection** -- Themes across rotations. Which executive perspectives repeatedly agree? Which repeatedly conflict?
**6c. Convergence Map** -- Where multiple positions agree on the same course of action.
**6d. Divergence Report** -- Which C-Suite perspectives fundamentally conflict for this challenge, and what that conflict reveals.
**6e. Blind Spot Report** -- What only one executive lens revealed that the others missed entirely.
**6f. Cross-Functional Impact Assessment** -- For each proposed action, map the impact across all six executive domains:
- Which functions benefit and which absorb cost
- Second-order effects across the org chart
- Where functional incentives misalign with the organizational recommendation
**6g. Resource Allocation Recommendation** -- Rank top 3 strategic options with:
- Required investment (capital, people, time, political capital)
- Expected return and timeline
- Organizational risk and mitigation
- Which executive functions must lead vs. support
**6h. Board-Ready Summary** -- A concise decision memo suitable for a board of directors:
- Situation (2 sentences)
- Recommendation (1 sentence)
- Key risks and mitigants (3 bullets)
- Resource ask (1 sentence)
- Decision required by (timeline)

## EXECUTION NOTES

1. **Every C-suite role sees something the others cannot.** The CEO sees the whole board. The CFO sees the math. The COO sees the machine. The CMO sees the customer. The CPO sees the product. The CTO sees the architecture. The synthesis is where the real decision lives.
2. **Honor the perspective.** Each face represents a real executive function with its own logic, incentives, and blind spots. Apply it as that executive would actually think, then let the cross-analysis find the synthesis.
3. **The organization is the Core.** Never lose sight of the specific company, stage, industry, and stakeholders. Every rotation should move closer to a specific, executable organizational decision.
4. **Specificity over generality.** "Improve alignment" is not an insight. "The CFO's capital allocation model assumes 18-month payback, but the CTO's platform migration requires 30 months -- the board must extend the investment horizon or reduce scope" is an insight.
