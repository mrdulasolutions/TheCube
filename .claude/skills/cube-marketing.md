# THE CUBE -- Marketing Stack

You are executing The Cube with the **Marketing Stack**. Six proven marketing strategy frameworks mapped to a Rubik's cube. The marketing challenge goes at the core. The cube rotates through 30 positions -- faces, edges, axes, corners -- producing a comprehensive multi-dimensional marketing analysis.

Each framework sees the customer, the market, and the opportunity from a different altitude. The rotations force you out of whatever marketing mental model you are stuck in.

### THE SIX FACES

**FACE 1 -- JOBS TO BE DONE (F) -- Clayton Christensen**
Customers do not buy products. They hire them to make progress in specific life circumstances. Innovation succeeds when it solves a job the customer is already trying to do. Shift from demographics to causation -- the *why* behind the purchase.
*"People don't want a quarter-inch drill. They want a quarter-inch hole."*
Self-prompt: "What job is the customer hiring this product to do? What progress are they trying to make? What are they firing to hire us?"

**FACE 2 -- POSITIONING (B) -- Al Ries & Jack Trout**
Marketing is a battle fought in the mind of the prospect, not in the marketplace. You win by owning a distinct word or idea in the customer's head relative to competitors. Perception is reality.
*"Positioning is not what you do to a product. It is what you do to the mind of the prospect."*
Self-prompt: "What word do we own in the customer's mind? What do competitors own? What space is available?"

**FACE 3 -- STORYBRAND (L) -- Donald Miller**
Customers are the hero of the story, not your brand. Your brand is the guide. Confusion kills conversions. Clarify your message using the 7-part story framework: a hero has a problem, meets a guide, who gives them a plan, calls them to action, that ends in success and avoids failure.
*"If you confuse, you lose."*
Self-prompt: "Is the customer the hero in our messaging? Are we the guide? Is our message clear enough that a caveman could understand it?"

**FACE 4 -- BLUE OCEAN STRATEGY (R) -- W. Chan Kim & Renee Mauborgne**
Stop competing in bloody red oceans. Create uncontested market space by simultaneously pursuing differentiation AND low cost through value innovation. Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create.
*"Make the competition irrelevant."*
Self-prompt: "What factors can we eliminate that the industry takes for granted? What can we raise well above the standard? What has never been offered?"

**FACE 5 -- CROSSING THE CHASM (U) -- Geoffrey Moore**
There is a lethal gap between early adopters and the pragmatist mainstream. Most products die in this chasm because these two groups buy for fundamentally different reasons. Target one beachhead niche with a whole product, then expand.
*"The chasm -- the gap between visionaries and pragmatists -- is where most tech products die."*
Self-prompt: "Where are we in the adoption lifecycle? Are we still selling to visionaries? What does the pragmatist mainstream need that early adopters did not?"

**FACE 6 -- GROWTH LOOPS (D) -- Andrew Chen / Reforge**
Sustainable growth comes from closed loops, not leaky funnels. The output of one cohort becomes the input that acquires the next. Growth is a system that compounds, not a campaign that depletes.
*"Funnels describe, loops explain."*
Self-prompt: "What is our growth loop? Does the output of our product naturally create new inputs? Where does the loop leak?"

### OPPOSITE FACE PAIRS (Rotation Axes)

- **X-Axis (F<>B):** Jobs to Be Done x Positioning -- **Truth vs Perception**. Christensen says customers hire products for real functional and emotional jobs — the causal "why" behind every purchase. Ries & Trout say marketing is a battle for the customer's mind — perception IS reality, regardless of the product's actual capability. One reveals what the customer truly needs; the other reveals what they believe they need. The gap between these is where most marketing goes wrong.

- **Y-Axis (U<>D):** Crossing the Chasm x Growth Loops -- **Adoption Barriers vs Growth Mechanics**. Moore warns that a lethal gap exists between early adopters and mainstream — most products die here because these groups buy for fundamentally different reasons. Chen/Reforge says growth comes from closed loops where one cohort's output becomes the next cohort's input. One explains why growth stalls; the other explains how growth compounds. The tension reveals whether you're stuck because the loop is broken or because the chasm is blocking it.

- **Z-Axis (L<>R):** StoryBrand x Blue Ocean -- **Clarify the Story vs Rewrite the Game**. Miller says confusion kills conversions — clarify your message so the customer instantly sees themselves as the hero of a story your brand guides. Kim & Mauborgne say stop competing — create uncontested market space through value innovation. One says tell your story better; the other says change the entire story. The tension reveals whether the market needs clearer communication or a fundamentally new category.

## EXECUTION PROTOCOL

Follow the same UX Protocol as the core Cube: opening banner (with "Marketing Stack" subtitle), progress indicators, Key Insight tags, phase transitions, cross-references, Complexity Assessment.

### STEP 0: PROBLEM INTAKE

If the user provided a marketing challenge, use it. Otherwise prompt:

> **What marketing challenge would you like to put inside The Cube?**
>
> Describe the product, market, growth problem, messaging challenge, or strategic question. Include: what you sell, who you sell to, current traction, and what is not working.

### STEP 1: CORE LOCK

```
## THE CORE

**Challenge:** [1-2 sentence crystallized marketing problem]
**Stakes:** [What is at risk -- growth, market position, burn rate, relevance]
**Current Approach:** [How you are currently marketing -- the frame The Cube will break out of]
**Market Context:** [B2B/B2C/DTC | Stage | Current traction | Key metric that is stuck]
```

### STEP 2: FACE SCAN (Positions 1-6)

4-8 sentences each. End with Key Insight.

**[F] Position 1: Jobs to Be Done** -- What job is the customer hiring this for? What competing solutions are they "firing"? What progress are they trying to make?
**[B] Position 2: Positioning** -- What word/idea do you own in the customer's mind? What do competitors own? Where is the open space?
**[L] Position 3: StoryBrand** -- Is the customer the hero? Are you the guide? Is the message clear? Run the 7-part framework.
**[R] Position 4: Blue Ocean** -- What can you Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create? Where is uncontested space?
**[U] Position 5: Crossing the Chasm** -- Where are you in the adoption lifecycle? What does the pragmatist mainstream need?
**[D] Position 6: Growth Loops** -- What is the growth loop? Where does it leak? What is the reinvestment mechanism?

Display Complexity Assessment after all 6 faces.

### STEP 3: EDGE ROTATIONS (Positions 7-18)

3-5 sentences each. Focus on emergent cross-insight. End with Key Insight.

#### [F-U] Position 7: "The Adoption Bridge" (JTBD + Crossing the Chasm)
*Christensen reveals the job customers hire you for. Moore warns that early adopters and mainstream hire for different reasons — the job itself may shift as you cross the chasm.*
**Prompt:** "Early adopters hired your product for one job. The mainstream may hire it for a different job entirely. What job does the pragmatist mainstream need done, and is your product actually solving it?"

#### [F-R] Position 8: "The Unmet Job" (JTBD + Blue Ocean)
*Christensen's JTBD surfaces jobs nobody is solving well. Kim & Mauborgne's Blue Ocean says uncontested space is where value is created. Together — unmet jobs ARE blue oceans.*
**Prompt:** "What job are customers struggling with that no competitor addresses? Where does JTBD analysis reveal unmet demand that maps to uncontested market space?"

#### [F-D] Position 9: "The Viral Job" (JTBD + Growth Loops)
*Christensen identifies the job. Chen asks: does completing that job naturally create the next customer? The best growth loops are built on jobs that are inherently shareable.*
**Prompt:** "Of the jobs your product does, which one naturally compels users to tell others? Where does the job itself create a growth loop?"

#### [F-L] Position 10: "The Hero's Need" (JTBD + StoryBrand)
*Christensen says customers hire products for progress. Miller says customers are heroes with a problem, seeking a guide. Together — frame the job as the hero's quest.*
**Prompt:** "The customer has a job to be done (JTBD). They are the hero (StoryBrand). How does the job map to the hero's external problem, internal frustration, and philosophical 'should'?"

#### [B-U] Position 11: "The Beachhead" (Positioning + Crossing the Chasm)
*Ries & Trout say own a word in the customer's mind. Moore says target one niche segment to cross the chasm. Together — own the position in the exact segment that crosses first.*
**Prompt:** "What position can you own in the specific beachhead segment that will carry you across the chasm? What word belongs to you in THAT niche's mind?"

#### [B-R] Position 12: "The Category Claim" (Positioning + Blue Ocean)
*Ries & Trout say the mind has limited slots and incumbents own the best ones. Kim & Mauborgne say create uncontested space. Together — position yourself in a category that doesn't exist yet, where every mental slot is open.*
**Prompt:** "If you create a blue ocean, you can define the positioning from scratch. What new category can you name and own? What word will you plant in a mind that has no incumbent?"

#### [B-D] Position 13: "The Growth Position" (Positioning + Growth Loops)
*Ries & Trout say your position must be memorable and distinct. Chen says growth comes from loops, not campaigns. Together — a position so clear it becomes part of how users describe themselves to others.*
**Prompt:** "What positioning creates natural shareability? What position makes users want to tell others? Where does your owned word become part of how customers describe their own identity?"

#### [B-L] Position 14: "The One-Liner" (Positioning + StoryBrand)
*Ries & Trout say own one idea. Miller says the customer must instantly understand the story. Together — one sentence that captures both your competitive position AND the hero's journey.*
**Prompt:** "Combine your competitive position with StoryBrand clarity. Write the one sentence that captures both where you sit in the market AND the hero's journey. If your customer had to explain you in one breath, what would they say?"

#### [U-L] Position 15: "The Bridge Story" (Crossing the Chasm + StoryBrand)
*Moore says visionaries and pragmatists buy for different reasons. Miller says the story must be clear. Together — how does the narrative evolve from 'this is revolutionary' to 'this is reliable'?*
**Prompt:** "Early adopters buy the vision. Pragmatists buy the proven solution. What story bridges these two audiences? How does the narrative evolve from 'this is revolutionary' to 'this is reliable'?"

#### [U-R] Position 16: "The New Market" (Crossing the Chasm + Blue Ocean)
*Moore says the chasm kills products competing in existing categories. Kim & Mauborgne say stop competing entirely. Together — what if the chasm disappears when there's no incumbent category to compare against?*
**Prompt:** "What if the chasm exists because you are competing in an existing category? If you created a new category (blue ocean), would the chasm disappear because pragmatists have nothing to compare you to?"

#### [D-L] Position 17: "The Story Loop" (Growth Loops + StoryBrand)
*Chen says growth compounds when output becomes input. Miller says the customer's hero journey drives action. Together — the hero's success story IS the acquisition channel for the next hero.*
**Prompt:** "How does the customer's hero journey itself become the growth loop? When the hero succeeds (using your product), does that success story naturally attract new heroes?"

#### [D-R] Position 18: "The Flywheel" (Growth Loops + Blue Ocean)
*Chen says growth loops compound. Kim & Mauborgne say uncontested markets have no friction from competitors. Together — a compounding loop with zero competitive drag.*
**Prompt:** "In blue ocean space with no competition, what growth loop compounds fastest? When you are the only option for a new job, how does each customer mechanically bring the next?"

### STEP 4: AXIS TENSIONS (Positions 19-21)

4-6 sentences each.

#### [X-Axis] Position 19: "The Perception Gap" (JTBD x Positioning)
*Truth vs Perception. What they need vs what they believe.*
**Prompt:** "JTBD reveals the real job. Positioning reveals the perceived category. How wide is the gap between what customers actually need and what they think they need? Is your product correctly positioned for the real job, or positioned for a job the market invented?"

#### [Y-Axis] Position 20: "The Growth Paradox" (Crossing the Chasm x Growth Loops)
*What blocks growth vs what compounds it.*
**Prompt:** "Growth Loops say growth should compound naturally. Crossing the Chasm says there is a structural barrier that prevents exactly that. Where is the growth loop breaking? Is the chasm the reason the loop does not close? Or is the missing loop the reason you cannot cross the chasm?"

#### [Z-Axis] Position 21: "The Category Question" (StoryBrand x Blue Ocean)
*Clarify your story vs rewrite the game.*
**Prompt:** "StoryBrand says clarify your message within the existing narrative. Blue Ocean says change the narrative entirely. Should you tell a clearer story in the current category, or create a new category that requires a completely new story? Which serves this product better?"

### STEP 5: CORNER ROTATIONS (Positions 22-29)

3-5 sentences each. Each corner picks one from each opposite pair.

#### [FUL] Position 22: "The Launch Story" (JTBD + Crossing the Chasm + StoryBrand)
*Right job + right segment + right narrative.*
**Prompt:** "You know the job (JTBD), the beachhead (Chasm), and the story (StoryBrand). What does the perfect launch look like? What is the story you tell to the first niche about the job you solve?"

#### [FUR] Position 23: "The Market Maker" (JTBD + Crossing the Chasm + Blue Ocean)
*Right job + adoption strategy + new category.*
**Prompt:** "What if you cross the chasm by solving a job nobody else addresses in a category that does not exist yet? What does the whole-product solution look like for a job in a blue ocean?"

#### [FDL] Position 24: "The Viral Narrative" (JTBD + Growth Loops + StoryBrand)
*Right job + growth loop + compelling story.*
**Prompt:** "The job creates the story. The story fuels the loop. The loop acquires new heroes with the same job. What does this self-reinforcing cycle look like? Where does it start?"

#### [FDR] Position 25: "The Rocketship" (JTBD + Growth Loops + Blue Ocean)
*Right job + compounding growth + new market.*
**Prompt:** "Unmet job + no competition + compounding growth = exponential trajectory. What needs to be true for this rocketship to launch? What is the single biggest assumption that could kill it?"

#### [BUL] Position 26: "The Brand Bridge" (Positioning + Crossing the Chasm + StoryBrand)
*Right position + adoption strategy + clear story.*
**Prompt:** "Your position is clear, your story is sharp, and you are targeting the right niche to cross the chasm. What does the brand look like that carries all three? What happens when the beachhead niche talks about you to the next segment?"

#### [BUR] Position 27: "The Category King" (Positioning + Crossing the Chasm + Blue Ocean)
*Own position + cross chasm + new market.*
**Prompt:** "Category Kings capture 76% of the economics. To become one: own a position, define a new category, and cross into the mainstream. What category can you create and dominate? What must be true?"

#### [BDL] Position 28: "The Growth Story" (Positioning + Growth Loops + StoryBrand)
*Position + loops + narrative.*
**Prompt:** "Your position feeds the story. The story feeds the loop. The loop reinforces the position. What does this self-reinforcing brand engine look like? Where is the flywheel fastest?"

#### [BDR] Position 29: "The Unicorn" (Positioning + Growth Loops + Blue Ocean)
*Position + loops + uncontested market.*
**Prompt:** "Owned position in uncontested space with compounding growth. This is the rarest and most valuable combination. What makes this possible for your product? What is the one thing that would make it impossible?"

## EXECUTION NOTES

1. **Markets are specific.** "Target millennials" is not a strategy. "Target first-time remote team leads at Series A startups who are hiring their third engineer" is a strategy.
2. **Honor the frameworks.** Each face represents a real, published methodology. Apply it as intended.
3. **Growth loops must be concrete.** "Viral growth" is not a loop. "User completes onboarding → shares template → new user signs up to use template → completes onboarding" is a loop.
4. **The 90-day outline matters.** The final strategy should be executable starting this week, not a theoretical positioning exercise.
